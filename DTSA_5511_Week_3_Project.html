Problem Statement

In this assignment, I implemented a deep learning Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) to identify whether a medical scan image does or does not contain a cancerous tumor. Using a multi-layered CNN with convolution, pooling, normalization, and flattening components, I achieved up to 88% accuracy and 65% testing accuracy in my best set of hyperparameter tuning. This degree of accuracy wouldn't be adequate as a substitute for proper medical review of lymph node scans, but it does demonstrate the CNN being able to capture at least some of the statistical signatures of cancer.

Exploratory Data Analysis

Before training my CNN model, I first performed an exploratory data analysis on the data set. Since the data came from a Kaggle competition (Reference 1), it was already in a relatively usable format. I didn't have to remove outliers or normalize in any way. Initially, I did look at the ratio of positive/negative samples to assess the degree to which the data set was imbalanced. It was not significantly so. Then, I plotted a handful of the images. It was not apparent to me (someone with no medical training) which ones had the cancer, but that's what the CNN will be for.

Modeling Building and Training

Next, I built a CNN that I trained on the training data set. In terms of CNN layer structure and ordering, I initally followed the Tensor Flow tutorial (Reference 6) and then experimented with my own modifications. For hyperparameter tuning, I compared a simpler model with 8, 16, 32, and 64 sized filters with a more involve model that doubled the number of 8-filter CNN layers and included more batch normalization and pooling. I hypothesized that the more complex model would perform better, and I specifically focused on making my additional layer being a coarser rather than a finer convolution since I didn't want it to overfit.

model0 = Sequential([
  layers.Rescaling(1./255, input_shape=(96, 96, 3)),
  layers.Conv2D(8, (3,3), activation='relu'),
  layers.Conv2D(16, (3,3), activation='relu'),
  layers.BatchNormalization(),
  layers.MaxPooling2D(pool_size=(3,3)),

  layers.Conv2D(32, (3,3), activation='relu'),

  layers.Conv2D(64, (3,3), activation='relu'),
  layers.BatchNormalization(),
  layers.MaxPooling2D(pool_size=(3,3)),

  layers.Flatten(),

  layers.Dense(64, activation='sigmoid'),
  layers.Dense(2, activation = 'softmax')
])

model0.compile(optimizer='adam',
              loss=tf.keras.losses.SparseCategoricalCrossentropy(from_logits=True),
              metrics=['accuracy'])
